### **1. Critical Risks & Preventative Measures**

#### **A. The "Bundled Consent" Trap (High Risk: TCPA & SMS)**

* **The Issue:** In **Section 3.1** and **5.1**, you state: *"By providing your phone number... you expressly consent to receive text messages/calls."*
* **Why it's risky:** Under the TCPA (Telephone Consumer Protection Act) and FCC rulings regarding AI voices, "marketing" calls/texts require **Prior Express Written Consent**. Courts have repeatedly ruled that a user simply typing a phone number into a form and clicking "Submit" (where the T&C is linked in the footer) is **insufficient** for marketing consent.
* **Prevention:**
* **Legal Fix:** You cannot bury consent in the T&C. You need a separate, **unchecked** checkbox on your sign-up forms.
* **Text Edit:** Update Section 3.1 to reference this separate action: *"By checking the SMS consent box on our web forms, you provide prior express written consent..."*



#### **B. Ambiguity on "Coming Soon" Products (Contract Risk)**

* **The Issue:** **Section 1.1** defines "Services" to include VexioHRM and VexioExpense. However, **Section 10** lists them as "(Coming Soon)."
* **Why it's risky:** If a user subscribes today based on the definition in 1.1, but the product doesn't exist (per Section 10), you are technically in breach of contract or liable for false advertising immediately upon their payment.
* **Prevention:**
* **Text Edit:** Remove unreleased products from the active definition in **Section 1.1** or explicitly label them as "Beta/In Development" with a clause stating *no warranty of availability* for those specific modules.



#### **C. AI Output Ownership (Commercial & IP Risk)**

* **The Issue:** **Section 1.5** (Intellectual Property) states that VexioHQ owns the "Services," but it is silent on who owns the **Output** (the reports, marketing copy, or leads generated by the AI).
* **Why it's risky:** Clients generally expect to own what they pay for. If VexioSales generates a lead list or VexioMarketing writes an email sequence, your current terms imply *you* might still own that content. This is a deal-breaker for B2B Enterprise clients.
* **Prevention:**
* **New Clause Needed:** Add a section explicitly assigning ownership of "Customer Content" and "AI Output" to the Customer, while reserving your right to the underlying "AI Models" and "Algorithms."



#### **D. Liability Cap is Too Low for B2B (Financial Risk)**

* **The Issue:** **Section 1.7** caps liability at **$100**.
* **Why it's risky:** You are selling **HRM** (Human Resources) and **Expense** tools. If your AI makes a mistake in an HR decision (discrimination lawsuit) or expense reporting (IRS audit), the damages will far exceed $100. A court may find a $100 cap "unconscionable" and throw it out entirely, leaving you with *unlimited* liability.
* **Prevention:**
* **Standard Practice:** Raise the cap to "12 months of fees paid." This is commercially reasonable and more likely to be upheld in court than a nominal $100 fee.
